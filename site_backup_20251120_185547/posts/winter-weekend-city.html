Winter Weekend City Playbook

Travel

By Jasmine L. · 7 min read

Day 1: Start at a bakery or café with big windows; warm up and map your loop. Hit a museum or gallery by 10 a.m. while it’s quiet. Lunch near your next stop to avoid long, cold walks. Golden hour: pick a bridge or river walk, then an early dinner with reservations to skip the line.

Day 2: Choose one neighborhood and stay there—markets, shops, a small exhibit. Plan a sauna or spa block mid-afternoon to thaw out. Evening: a concert, bookstore, or speakeasy within a 10-minute walk of your stay so you’re not chasing transit in the cold.

Packing: merino base layer, compact insulated jacket, hat/gloves, waterproof boots you can walk in, and a small umbrella. Download maps offline and keep transit cards topped up. Bring a flat power bank; cold drains batteries fast.

Food: book one anchor meal; leave the rest flexible. Keep one emergency snack in your pocket so you’re not forced into the nearest tourist trap when temps drop.
