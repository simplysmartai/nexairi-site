College Football Playoff 2025-26: First-Round Matchups Deep Dive

As the leaves turn and the air gets crisp, college football fans gear up for the most exciting time of the year: the College Football Playoff (CFP). This season, the expanded playoff format has given more teams a shot at the national title, and that means more thrilling matchups that could change the landscape of college football. Whether you’re a die-hard fan or just getting into the sport, the first round of the 2025-26 CFP is packed with intrigue, rivalries, and the potential for upsets. Let’s dive deep into the four exciting matchups scheduled for December 19-20, 2025, and see what we can expect from these battles on the gridiron.

#9 Alabama at #8 Oklahoma

Date/Time/TV: December 19, 8:00 PM ET, ABC/ESPN
Location: Gaylord Family Oklahoma Memorial Stadium, Norman, OK

Team Profiles

The Alabama Crimson Tide finished the season with a 10-3 record, solidifying their reputation as a powerhouse in the SEC. Known for their strong defensive tradition, Alabama ranks in the top 10 nationally in scoring defense, allowing an average of just 18 points per game. Their offense, while not as explosive as in past years, still manages to put up around 28 points per game, leaning heavily on their ground game.

The Oklahoma Sooners also wrapped up the season at 10-2, showcasing a brand of football characterized by an explosive offense and a defense that has improved significantly throughout the year. Oklahoma ranks in the top 15 for scoring offense, averaging about 32 points per game, and boasts a solid defensive unit that has allowed 24 points per game.

Key Players to Watch

Alabama: Keep an eye on their star quarterback, who is not just a passing threat but also a capable rusher, making him a dual-threat nightmare for defenses. The leading rusher, averaging over 100 yards per game, is crucial to Alabama's ground game. Defensively, their double-digit sack defensive end will be key in pressuring Oklahoma's quarterback.

Oklahoma: Their quarterback, known for efficiency and mobility, can exploit defenses with both his arm and legs. Additionally, the Sooners feature a 1,000-yard rusher who can break off big plays. Defensively, their aggressive secondary will be tasked with containing Alabama's passing attack.

Coaching Matchup

Alabama’s head coach is known for a balanced offensive scheme that emphasizes power running and play-action passing, while also maintaining a defense that thrives on aggressive play from their front seven. Oklahoma's head coach, an offensive-minded innovator, adopts a quick-tempo spread offense that looks to create mismatches and capitalize on defensive lapses.

Injury Updates

Alabama’s starting wide receiver is questionable due to a minor ankle injury, but indications suggest he’ll likely play. Oklahoma will be missing their top linebacker for the season, which puts pressure on their defensive depth.

The Narrative

This matchup marks the ninth meeting between these two storied programs, with Oklahoma holding a slight edge in the series at 5-1-2. Alabama's rich history in the CFP contrasts with Oklahoma's recent consistency in playoff appearances, making this matchup not just a battle of teams, but a clash of legacies.

Prediction Factors

For Alabama to win, they’ll need to leverage their defense to pressure Oklahoma’s quarterback, forcing turnovers and controlling the game tempo. Oklahoma, on the other hand, must establish their offensive rhythm early, utilizing their dual-threat capabilities to stretch Alabama’s defense.

Expert Predictions

Experts are split on this one, but many lean towards Alabama due to their defensive strength and home-field advantage. The win probability stands at approximately 52% for Alabama and 48% for Oklahoma.

#10 Miami at #7 Texas A&M

Date/Time/TV: December 20, 12:00 PM ET, ABC/ESPN
Location: Kyle Field, College Station, TX

Team Profiles

The Miami Hurricanes come into this matchup as ACC champions, boasting a 10-2 record. Their defense ranks among the top 15 nationally, allowing only 20 points per game. Miami’s offense is balanced, averaging around 30 points per game, anchored by a strong rushing attack.

The Texas A&M Aggies, also with a 10-2 record, are known for their physical style of play, ranking in the top 10 for scoring offense at 34 points per game while maintaining a solid defense that allows just over 21 points per game.

Key Players to Watch

Miami: Their efficient quarterback is a key player, capable of making throws under pressure. The lead running back boasts over 1,200 rushing yards, while the defensive end leads the team in sacks, making him a constant threat to opposing quarterbacks.

Texas A&M: The Aggies’ dual-threat quarterback can change the game with both his arm and legs. Their power running back is a workhorse, while their deep-threat receiver can stretch the field and create mismatches.

Coaching Matchup

Miami’s head coach emphasizes a balanced offensive approach, integrating a strong run-pass mix, while their defense focuses on speed and coverage. Texas A&M's head coach prefers a run-heavy, physical offense, complemented by a defense built on toughness and pressure.
