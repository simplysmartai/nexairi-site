Compress Documents to 1% Size—Apple's New AI Does It Without Losing Meaning

AI can't read your 500-page PDF. Apple just fixed that forever.

The Problem Apple Solved

ChatGPT has a "context window" - max words it can read at once. Your 100-page report? Too big. Apple researchers said: "Compress it first."

CLaRa (Continuous Latent Reasoning Architecture) turns documents into tiny memory tokens. 128x smaller. But keeps every important fact.

How It Works (Super Simple)

  1. Semantic Compressor: Reads your PDF → extracts 16 "memory tokens" (think document DNA)
  2. Query Reasoner: Your question → matching memory tokens
  3. Answer Generator: Perfect response using 1/128th the space

Result? Mistral 7B (CLaRa's base) beats full-text RAG by 6+ F1 points at 16x compression.

Real Numbers
