Make-Ahead Everything: What to cook 48h out, chill, and reheat

By Sarah R. · 7 min read

Hosting Thanksgiving doesn’t mean you have to live in the kitchen for 48 straight hours. The real trick is knowing what you can cook in advance, stash in the fridge, and bring back to life on Thursday without anyone knowing you slept in. This guide rounds up make-ahead stars you can prep one to two days early so Thanksgiving Day is about small touch-ups, not full-scale battles.

Below are dishes that hold well in the fridge, reheat like champs, and still feel special on the table. For each one, you’ll see a quick overview plus a link to a trusted recipe with full instructions. Use this as your 48-hour “calm before the storm” menu.

Make-Ahead Creamy Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are the comfort backbone of most Thanksgiving plates, but trying to mash twenty pounds of spuds right before serving is chaos. This make-ahead method lets you prepare the potatoes up to two days in advance, then reheat them gently with cream, butter, or stock so they’re silky and steamy when it counts. Perfect if you want the “grandma made these” effect without the last-minute stovetop sprint.

Full recipe: Make-Ahead Mashed Potatoes (The Kitchn)

Make-Ahead Turkey Gravy

If gravy stresses you out more than relatives do, make it before anyone even arrives. This approach uses turkey wings and aromatics to build a deeply flavored stock that can be turned into gravy a day or two before Thanksgiving. On the big day, you simply reheat and thin with pan drippings, which means you’re whisking in peace instead of under pressure.

Full recipe: Make-Ahead Turkey Gravy (Simply Recipes)

Make-Ahead Sweet Potato Casserole

This sweet potato casserole hits that classic holiday note without demanding any day-of attention beyond sliding it into the oven. You can mash, season, and top the sweet potatoes with marshmallows or a pecan streusel up to 48 hours in advance. On Thanksgiving, bake until the center is warm and the top is toasted and crisp — no peeling or mashing required at 10 a.m.

Full recipe: Make-Ahead Sweet Potato Casserole (Melanie Cooks)

Classic Make-Ahead Stuffing

Stuffing (or dressing, depending on your region) is one of the easiest sides to move off your Thursday cook list. This version lets you cube and dry the bread ahead of time, then sauté your aromatics and assemble the mixture the day before. On Thanksgiving, bake it covered until piping hot, then uncover to crisp the edges while the turkey rests.

Full recipe: Make-Ahead Stuffing (Bon Appétit)

Make-Ahead Green Bean Casserole

Green bean casserole is pure nostalgia and also extremely friendly to advance prep. This recipe has you blanch the beans and cook the mushroom sauce early, then combine everything in a baking dish and chill. On Thanksgiving, you’ll just stir, top with crispy onions, and bake until everything is bubbling and browned.

Full recipe: Make-Ahead Green Bean Casserole (Spend With Pennies)

Roasted Root Vegetables (Reheat Friendly)

Roasted carrots, parsnips, and sweet potatoes bring color and sweetness to the table, and they’re surprisingly forgiving when it comes to reheating. Par-roast the vegetables a day ahead until just tender, then chill them on a sheet pan. On Thanksgiving, blast them in a hot oven to re-crisp the edges and wake the caramelized flavors back up.

Full recipe: Roasted Root Vegetables (Love & Lemons)

Make-Ahead Cranberry Sauce

Cranberry sauce is basically holiday jam, which means it loves fridge time. This simple sauce can be made days in advance and tucked away until the turkey is carved, and the flavors deepen as it sits. Serve it chilled or at room temperature, and enjoy the fact that one bright, tangy part of the meal is completely finished before guests arrive.

Full recipe: Simple Cranberry Sauce (Inspired Taste)

Make-Ahead Pumpkin Pie

Pies are the poster child for make-ahead desserts. This pumpkin pie can be baked one to two days in advance, cooled completely, and stored in the fridge. On Thanksgiving, bring it back to room temperature and add whipped cream just before serving, leaving your oven free for the last savory dishes.

Full recipe: Pumpkin Pie (Sally’s Baking Addiction)
