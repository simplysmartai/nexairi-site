Part 2: The Grain Library

Once you have a mill, you don’t need to turn your pantry into a mini grain elevator. You just need to understand the four pillars of wheat. Here is exactly what to stock.

Soft White Wheat: The Pastry Specialist

Ideal For: Cakes, cookies, crackers, pastries, and Asian noodles

Think of this as the "tenderizer." Soft white wheat has low protein, which means very little gluten development. It creates a fine, velvety flour that is terrible for yeast bread but incredible for delicate crumbs. If you want a biscuit that flakes or a cookie that melts, this is your grain.

Soft Red Wheat: The Snacking Grain

Ideal For: Crackers, pastries, breakfast cereals, flatbreads

A close cousin to Soft White but with a slightly higher protein count and a distinctive reddish hue from the bran. It’s versatile enough for some flatbreads but still leans toward tenderness. It adds a savory, cracker-like depth that white wheats miss.

Shop Soft Red at Dave's Farms

Hard White Wheat: The Crowd Pleaser

Ideal For: Everyday bread, pizza dough, sandwich loaves

This is the powerhouse. It has high protein content for strong gluten structure (so your bread rises high), but it lacks the bitter tannins found in red wheat. The result is a golden, mild-tasting whole wheat loaf that even picky eaters usually love. It bridges the gap between nutrition and approachable flavor.

Shop Hard White at Dave's Farms

Hard Red Wheat: The Artisan Standard

Ideal For: Rustic hearth loaves, sourdough, pasta

The workhorse. Hard Red boasts the highest protein for the strongest elastic doughs. It yields a rich, dark, nutty loaf with that classic "wheaty" bitterness that defines artisan bread. If you want a loaf with serious character and chew, this is the grain you reach for.

Part 3: A Simple Starter Pantry List

To keep things easy, here’s a simple starter list that covers a surprising amount of baking:

  • 10 lbs Hard White Wheat (For everyday bread)
  • 5 lbs Hard Red Wheat (For mixing into rustic loaves)
  • 5 lbs Soft White Wheat (For cookies/muffins)
  • Optional: 2 lbs Rye or Spelt for flavor experiments

With just these, you can make almost anything. You can get fancy with Einkorn and Kamut later.
