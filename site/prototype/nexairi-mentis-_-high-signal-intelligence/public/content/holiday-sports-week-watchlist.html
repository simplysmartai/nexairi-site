Holiday Sports Week 2025: The Games That Actually Matter

By James S. · 10 min read

Holiday sports are about two things: meaningful games and shared screens. This guide cuts through the clutter of the 2025 slate to highlight the matchups that will define the playoff picture across the NFL, College Football, and NBA.

NFL Thanksgiving: The Traditional Slate

  • 12:30 PM ET: Lions vs. Bears
    Detroit is chasing the #1 seed in the NFC, while Chicago’s young offense is fighting for a Wild Card spot. Expect high scoring and divisional grit.
  • 4:30 PM ET: Cowboys vs. Giants
    A classic NFC East rivalry. Dallas needs this win to stay relevant in the division race.
  • 8:20 PM ET: Dolphins vs. Packers
    The nightcap features two of the league's most explosive offenses in a potential Super Bowl preview. Speed in the cold at Lambeau.

Black Friday Football

  • 3:00 PM ET: Jets vs. Raiders
    Amazon’s exclusive Black Friday game. A grimy, defensive battle perfect for post-shopping recovery.

College Football: Rivalry Week (The Ones That Count)

  • The Game: Michigan vs. Ohio State (Saturday, Noon ET)
    As always, likely deciding the Big Ten East and a CFP berth. The intensity here is unrivaled.
  • The Iron Bowl: Alabama vs. Auburn (Saturday, 3:30 PM ET)
    Even if records are lopsided, weird things happen at Jordan-Hare. A must-watch for chaos lovers.
  • Florida vs. Florida State (Saturday Night)
    A sunshine state battle with major recruiting implications.

NBA: Early Form Checks

  • Thanksgiving Eve: Lakers vs. Warriors
    LeBron vs. Curry. The league always schedules this heavy hitter before the holiday break. A litmus test for the West.
  • Celtics vs. Bucks
    Eastern Conference supremacy on the line. Watch for defensive adjustments in the fourth quarter.

How to watch sanely with family

  • Pick two priority windows; announce early and stick to them.
  • Second‑screen quietly with captions; keep volume reasonable.
  • Make it social: prop cards, squares, or a simple bingo keeps casual viewers engaged.

Setup checklist

  • Internet stable (reboot router day‑before), streaming logins verified.
  • Spare HDMI cable, remote batteries, captions on by default.
  • Soft throw on every seat—comfort keeps viewers happy.

Enjoy the games—and the company. The wins are sweeter when the room’s happy too.
