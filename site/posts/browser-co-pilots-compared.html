Browser Co-Pilots Compared

Latency tests, privacy modes, and cost breakdowns for five AI sidebars.

Home Our Mission Lifestyle Travel Technology Sports Sandbox Contact
By Michael T. · 7 min read
software analysis

We tested five AI sidebar co-pilots for latency, memory footprint, and privacy controls. Two patterns emerged: lightweight co-pilots that summarize and cite cleanly, and heavy ones that try to do everything at the cost of RAM.

Baseline checks: does it run locally for quick actions, does it respect container tabs, and can you export cleanly to your notes tool without dragging styling cruft? If a co-pilot chokes when you open more than 10 tabs, it's off the list.

Pick one tool per mode: research (fast, cite-friendly), drafting (good at structure and tone), and fact checking (strict about sources). Pay for only what you use daily; sunset the rest to keep your machine—and your focus—fast.
