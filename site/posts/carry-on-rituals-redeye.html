Carry-On Rituals for Red-Eye Routes

Travel

By Noah V. · 5 min read

Build a repeatable overnight kit: compression socks, a soft neck wrap, and a mini sleep mask. Keep it in a clear pouch so you can grab it without re-packing your whole bag. Include a tiny moisturizer and lip balm—cabin air is relentless.

Eat light, hydrate early, and set a 90-minute timer after takeoff to dim screens and swap into your sleep kit. Save one upbeat playlist for landing so your brain knows the flight is over. If you can, book an aisle so you can stand every few hours without climbing over anyone.

On arrival, move: 10 minutes of walking, water, and daylight. You're not trying to beat jet lag—just trimming the worst of it so you can enjoy the first day. Keep a simple ritual for hotel arrivals: unpack only the essentials, set out tomorrow's clothes, and note one local spot to visit before noon.
