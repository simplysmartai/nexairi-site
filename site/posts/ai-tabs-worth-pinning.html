AI Tabs Worth Pinning

Technology

By Noah V. · 6 min read

Keep only three AI helpers open: one for research, one for drafting, one for fact-checks. Anything beyond that becomes noise. Measure them on latency, accuracy, and how well they export to your stack—markdown, docs, or CMS.

Set window rules: research in a dedicated profile, drafting in a clean tab set with notifications off, and fact checks running in a stripped-down extension. If a tool spikes CPU or memory, it's not worth pinning. Archive experiments weekly so you don't pile up half-tested betas.
