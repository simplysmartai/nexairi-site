Desk Stack: Silent but Smart

Technology

By Michael T. · 6 min read

Build around silence and local control. Pick a low-profile keyboard under 45 dB, a mouse that doesn't click loudly, and ambient sensors that only push alerts you actually need. Cable everything with braided ties and a single under-desk tray so your sightlines stay clean.

Automation rules: auto-mute calls when the door sensor opens, shift lights to 4000K after sunset, and pause notifications when a focus timer runs. If a device can't run locally or spikes CPU, it's out. Run monthly firmware checks and snapshot your configs so a failure takes minutes to recover, not hours.

Finally, cap the stack: one personal AI tab, one work AI tab, and one fact-check tool. Latency and memory footprints matter as much as clever features. The quieter the stack, the more you'll actually ship.
