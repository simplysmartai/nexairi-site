Holiday Post-Game Reset

Sports

By Noah V. · 6 min read

TL/TD

Three anchors: hydration math, sleep guardrails, and light movement the morning after. Holidays are for joy, not slow-motion burnout.

Hydration: one liter of water plus electrolytes post-game, then another across the next morning. Cap caffeine after 2 p.m. if you’re watching late kickoffs—sleep quality beats one more cup.

Sleep: darken the room, drop temp a couple degrees, and use a short wind-down (box breathing, light reading) even if bed is later than normal. If you’re crashing at family, pack a sleep mask and soft earplugs.

Movement: 10–15 minutes the next day—mobility, calf/hip work, light walk. If you’ve been sitting through multiple games, add two sets of bodyweight squats and wall slides to reset posture.

Food: go balanced at the next meal—protein, colorful veg, slow carbs. Avoid the “double fried” spiral after back-to-back watch parties; it wrecks energy for days.

Schedule: block one quiet hour after the game stack. No calls, no chores. Let your system settle so you’re not dragging the vibe into the next day.
