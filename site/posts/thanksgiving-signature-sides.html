Signature Thanksgiving Sides That Steal the Spotlight

Crowd-loved, calm-to-execute sides from across regions and diets—no chaos required.

By Sarah R. · 7 min read

Thanksgiving sides carry the meal: they balance the turkey, keep guests happy while you carve, and travel well if you’re a guest. Here’s a calm, mixed-diet lineup—classic, vegan-friendly, and regionally varied—plus links to trusted recipes so you can cook with confidence.

Crisp-Top Cornbread Stuffing (no soggy middle)

Golden edges and a custardy middle give this stuffing true main-character energy. The mix of cornbread and country loaf keeps it tender without turning mushy, while sage and thyme make the whole kitchen smell like a hug. Broil for a minute so every bite has a crisp corner that guests fight over. It lands warm, rustic, and ready to soak up gravy.

Recipe: Serious Eats cornbread stuffing

Brown Butter Mashed Potatoes (make-ahead friendly)

Nutty brown butter turns simple potatoes into something you linger over. They’re silky without feeling heavy, and they stay lovely in a warm oven while you plate the turkey. A gloss of olive oil on top catches the light and keeps the surface from drying out. It’s the side everyone reaches for first—and again.

Recipe: Love & Lemons mashed potatoes

Roasted Brussels with Maple-Chile Glaze (vegan)

These Brussels walk the sweet-heat line perfectly: deep char on the cut sides, then a maple-chile-lime glaze that wakes everything up. Toasted pecans add a buttery crunch so even the Brussels skeptics keep nibbling. They’re vegan, bright, and surprising enough to earn space next to the turkey. Great hot, still good at room temp.

Recipe: Bon Appétit brussels with chile-lime

Garlic Miso Green Beans (umami, dairy-free)

Snappy beans coated in garlicky miso taste like a clean, savory “reset” on a full plate. A quick blanch, then a flash in the pan with miso and rice vinegar gives them depth without cream or butter. Sesame seeds on top add texture and a nutty aroma. They’re light, bright, and disappear fast.

Recipe: Just One Cookbook sesame green beans

Roasted Roots with Tahini Drizzle (vegan, travels well)

Carrots, parsnips, and sweet potatoes roast until their edges caramelize and the insides turn candy-sweet. A lemony tahini drizzle and fresh parsley cut through the richness, so each bite feels balanced. It packs and reheats beautifully, making it a stellar “I’ll bring a side” dish. The color mix also brightens the table.

Recipe: Ottolenghi-style roasted roots

Parker House Rolls (classic, pillowy)

Parker House rolls are the warm welcome everyone hopes for: soft, buttery layers that pull apart in steam. Mix the dough the night before, then bake day-of so the whole room smells like a bakery. A brush of salted butter and flaky salt on top keeps them glossy and irresistible. They make leftover sandwiches feel fancy.

Recipe: NYT Parker House rolls

Citrus-Cranberry Relish (bright, 5 minutes)

Fresh cranberries, whole orange, and a hit of lime become a ruby relish that wakes up every heavy dish on the table. It’s five minutes in the food processor and zero stovetop time, so it won’t hog a burner. Serve it chilled for a cool, tart contrast. Leftovers are great on turkey sandwiches the next day.

Recipe: Simply Recipes cranberry relish

Make-Ahead Gravy with Umami Backbone

This gravy tastes like you babysat it all day, but it’s built from roasted veg stock and a spoon of soy or miso for depth. Make it a day or two ahead, then reheat gently while the turkey rests so there’s no last-minute panic. Strain for a silk-smooth pour and finish with a knob of butter. It anchors every plate and keeps for three days chilled.

Recipe: Serious Eats make-ahead gravy

Service Plan (staggered timing)

  • Day before: bake cornbread, make relish, brown butter, miso green bean sauce, gravy base.
  • Morning of: par-roast roots, blanch beans, proof rolls.
  • Final hour: roast sprouts, finish roots, reheat potatoes, bake rolls, warm gravy.

Plating tips (calm table, quick resets)

  • Wide shallow platters keep heat better than deep bowls.
  • Label vegan and gluten-free dishes with small cards.
  • Keep a small “hot landing zone” by the oven for quick swaps.
