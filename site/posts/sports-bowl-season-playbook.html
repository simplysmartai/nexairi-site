College Bowl Season Playbook

Schedules, matchups, and sanity tricks so you catch the right games without burning every weekend.

By Rally · 8–10 min read

TL;DR

  • Pick 6–8 must‑watch games; everything else is highlights.
  • Block quality windows (New Year’s Six + your team + 1 upset pick).
  • Use split‑screen and 30‑minute check‑ins to avoid doom scrolling.
  • Prep snacks and halftime walks; the body remembers.
  • Know your opt‑outs and coaching changes; adjust expectations.

Build a watch list that respects your time

There are too many bowls to watch them all. Start with your team, add the New Year’s Six, then one or two upset candidates. That’s eight games—enough football without turning your break into a blur.

Matchup notes that actually help

  • Opt‑outs: star players skipping the bowl can flip a line; check beat reporters 48 hours out.
  • Coaching changes: interim staffs simplify game plans—lean under on totals unless both QBs are intact.
  • Trenches travel: lines hold up in any stadium; if you love a run game, you’ll love December.

House rules for healthier weekends

Use split‑screen for early windows. At halftime: stretch, water, snack, quick tidy. If a game goes sideways by mid‑third, swap to highlights and move on. Your December self will thank you.
