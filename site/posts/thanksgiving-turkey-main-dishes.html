Thanksgiving Turkey and Wonderful Other Main Dishes

By Sarah R. · 7 min read

Perfect Dry-Brined Roast Turkey

Achieve incredibly crispy skin and succulent, juicy meat with this fuss-free dry-brined turkey method. The salt penetrates the meat, seasoning it from within and ensuring an even cook, making for an unforgettable holiday centerpiece. Roast a 12-14 lb bird at 425°F for 30 min, then 325°F for 2-2.5 hours, aiming for 160-165°F in the breast and 170-175°F in the thigh. For best results, chill the turkey unwrapped for 2-3 days after brining, and always let it rest for 30-40 minutes before carving to redistribute juices.

Speedy Spatchcock Turkey with Herbs

Cut down your cooking time significantly and ensure every bite is tender and flavorful with a spatchcocked turkey. By removing the backbone and flattening the bird, it cooks more evenly and develops beautifully crisp skin. Roast a 12-16 lb turkey at 450°F for 80-100 minutes until breast reaches 160-165°F and thigh hits 170-175°F. To transport, carve the turkey and pack tightly in foil, reheating gently with a splash of broth. Leftovers chill well for up to 3 days.

Classic Herb-Butter Roasted Turkey

Embrace tradition with this aromatic herb-butter roasted turkey, guaranteeing a moist interior and a stunning, golden-brown exterior. The herb-infused butter bastes the skin as it cooks, creating incredible flavor and tenderness. Roast a 10-12 lb turkey at 325°F for 2.5-3 hours, or until the breast reaches 160-165°F and the thigh 170-175°F. Always allow your bird to rest for 20-30 minutes before carving to lock in those delicious juices. For travel, carve and pack tightly, reheating gently with a little pan drippings.

Rich Make-Ahead Turkey Gravy

Elevate your holiday meal with a robust, make-ahead turkey gravy that frees up valuable kitchen time on the big day. Crafted from roasted turkey wings or necks, this gravy develops deep, savory flavors that complement any main dish. Simmer for at least an hour, then strain and chill for up to 3 days, or freeze for a month. Reheat gently on the stovetop, whisking in pan drippings from your freshly roasted turkey for an extra layer of flavor.

Elegant Roasted Salmon with Lemon & Dill

Offer a stunning, lighter alternative to turkey with this vibrant roasted salmon, brightened by fresh lemon and fragrant dill. It's incredibly quick to prepare, perfect for smaller gatherings or as a delicious second main. Roast a side of salmon on a sheet pan at 400°F for 12-15 minutes, or until flaky. Transport chilled in an airtight container and re-crisp briefly in a hot oven (350°F for 5-7 min) or serve at room temperature.

Hearty Vegetarian Mushroom Wellington

Impress your guests with this magnificent Mushroom Wellington, a savory vegetarian centerpiece that's rich in flavor and texture. A medley of mushrooms, herbs, and seasonings is encased in flaky puff pastry, creating a show-stopping dish. Prepare the filling and assemble the wellington up to a day in advance, chilling until ready to bake at 400°F for 30-35 minutes until golden. Reheat slices gently in the oven to maintain pastry crispness.

Whole Roasted Cauliflower with Tahini Sauce

This whole roasted cauliflower makes an incredible, visually stunning vegan centerpiece that's bursting with flavor and texture. The cauliflower softens beautifully in the oven, developing a delicate sweetness that pairs perfectly with a tangy tahini dressing. Roast a whole cauliflower at 400°F for 45-60 minutes until tender and caramelized. It travels well chilled, and can be gently reheated in the oven, or served at room temperature drizzled with the sauce.

Savory Vegan Nut Roast

For a truly satisfying plant-based main, this savory vegan nut roast delivers on all fronts: hearty, flavorful, and wonderfully festive. Packed with nutritious nuts, vegetables, and herbs, it offers a fantastic alternative to traditional roasts. Bake at 375°F for 40-50 minutes until golden and firm. The nut roast can be prepared and assembled a day in advance, then baked just before serving, or baked and gently reheated covered in foil.

Flavorful Smoked Turkey

Introduce incredible depth of flavor and free up your oven with a succulent smoked turkey this holiday season. The slow smoking process yields incredibly tender meat with a beautiful smoky crust, a delightful twist on tradition. Smoke a 10-12 lb turkey at 225-250°F for 4-6 hours, or until breast reaches 160-165°F and thigh 170-175°F. Rest for 30-40 minutes. For travel, carve and wrap tightly in foil, reheating gently in the oven with a little broth to retain moisture.

Hearty Plant-Based Lentil Loaf

This comforting and hearty lentil loaf is an excellent, protein-rich main for any plant-based holiday table. Made with simple, wholesome ingredients, it's incredibly satisfying and can be easily customized with your favorite herbs and spices. Bake in a loaf pan at 375°F for 45-55 minutes until firm and golden. This loaf can be fully prepared and baked ahead of time, then sliced and gently reheated in the oven, making it perfect for potlucks or stress-free entertaining.
